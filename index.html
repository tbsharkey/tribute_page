Marvin Minsky

The Father of Artificial Intelligence

Marvin Minsky was an American scientist who specialized in artificial intelligence. Born in New York in 1927 he went on to serve in the US Navy in 1944-45 before starting college. He started working at MIT in 1958 and is the founder of the MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory. He worked at MIT until his death in 2016.
Mr. Minsky was known as a pioneer in artificial intelligence. He believed that the human mind is no different from a computer. He believed that intelligence emerged from a series of numerous and simple agents rather than one system. His work inspired generations of students and his fundamental work in computer science and AI have helped create the modern world that we live in. Mr. Minsky passed away in January 2016 at the age of 88.

Education

  • Havard: BA in Mathamatics 1950
  • Princenton: PHD in Mathamatics 1954

Accomplishments

  • Invented the confocal scanning microscope, an instrument that is still widely used today in medical and scientific research.
  • Developed mechanical arms which were a precursor to robots.
  • Built "SNARC" (Stochastic Neural Analog Reinforcement Calculator) —considered the first artificial learning machine.
  • Co-founded the MIT Artificial Intelligence Project. This lab was a part of ARPAnet which was a precursor to the internet.
  • Advised the director of 2001 A Space Odyssey. His work helped create HAL 9000 which is the most notorious AI in film history.
  • Developed "The Society of Mind" theory of human intelligence which was based on research in developmental child psychology and artificial intelligence.
